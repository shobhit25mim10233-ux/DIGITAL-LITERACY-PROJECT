Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud Incident

Rohit, a college student in Bhopal, decided to sell his old smartphone on an online marketplace.
Soon after posting the advertisement, he received a call from a person claiming to be an interested buyer.
The caller agreed to pay immediately and sent Rohit a QR code, stating that scanning it would help him receive the payment.
Trusting the buyer, Rohit scanned the QR code using his UPI app.
He was then prompted to enter his UPI PIN. Assuming it was necessary to receive the money, he entered the PIN.
Within seconds, ₹10,000 was debited from his bank account instead of being credited.
When Rohit tried contacting the buyer again, the number was switched off.
Realizing he had been scammed, he immediately informed his bank and reported the incident to the national cybercrime helpline (1930) and the official cybercrime portal.
This case highlights a common UPI fraud technique where scammers trick users into scanning QR codes and entering their PIN.
It emphasizes the importance of understanding that receiving money does not require entering a UPI PIN.
Users must stay alert, verify transactions carefully, and avoid trusting unknown individuals in online transactions.
